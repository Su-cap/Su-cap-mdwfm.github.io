From Glance to Inspect: Frontier Maps from Adaptive Weighting of Multi-Dimensional Cues for Zero-Shot Object Navigation

Abstract

In unfamiliar environments, object-oriented navigation poses a significant challenge for robotic systems, particularly in the context of zero-shot object navigation. Previous methods typecially attempted to extract cues with image detection or vision-language models directly, which were limited in the utilization of relationships between the cues and the target object. To address this limitation, we propose a novel zero-shot object navigation method called Multi-Dimensional-Weighted Frontier Maps (MDWFM). This approach adaptively analyzes the correlations among task cues, scene cues, and geometric cues, selecting the most promising frontier by dynamically weighting scores across different dimensions. This strategy mitigates the distraction caused by irrelevant visual cues in long-distance tasks, leading to more accurate target identification. Our method employs a lightweight visual model, avoiding the use of vision-language models (VLMs) or large language models (LLMs), yet achieves superior performance by providing a more nuanced understanding of scene information. We evaluate MDWFM in photo-realistic environments using the Habitat-Matterport 3D (HM3D) and Gibson datasets within the Habitat simulator. Our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on both datasets, as measured by success weighted by path length (SPL) and success rate (SR) for the Object Goal Navigation task.
